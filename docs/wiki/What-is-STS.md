# 🧬 What is Sensory Tracer Science (STS)?

**Sensory Tracer Science (STS)** is a revolutionary physics-based framework that governs how sensory information propagates through biological systems while respecting fundamental physical laws. It bridges the gap between theoretical physics and practical biological applications.

## 🎯 **Core Concept**

STS provides a **unified theoretical framework** for understanding and designing sensory systems that:

- **Conserve energy** according to thermodynamic principles
- **Preserve information** following information theory
- **Respect causality** within relativistic constraints  
- **Honor quantum limits** of measurement precision
- **Maintain biological compatibility** within living systems

## ⚡ **The Five Fundamental Axioms**

STS is founded on **five non-negotiable axioms** that any sensory system must satisfy:

### **A1: Energy Conservation (Landauer's Principle)**
```
ΔE_min ≥ k_B T ln(2) per bit erased
```
- **Principle**: All sensory processes must respect thermodynamic energy limits
- **Implication**: Cannot create or destroy energy, only transform it
- **Biology**: Cellular ATP budgets constrain tracer operations

### **A2: Information Conservation**  
```
I_total = I_detected + I_lost (no creation/destruction)
```
- **Principle**: Information cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed
- **Implication**: Total information content is conserved through transformations
- **Biology**: Neural signals preserve information content during propagation

### **A3: Causality (Relativistic Constraint)**
```
v_signal ≤ c/n (speed of light in medium)
```
- **Principle**: Information cannot propagate faster than light in the medium
- **Implication**: Signal speed is physically limited
- **Biology**: Diffusion and transport rates have upper bounds

### **A4: Quantum Measurement Limits**
```
Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2 (Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle)
```
- **Principle**: All measurements are subject to quantum mechanical constraints
- **Implication**: Perfect measurement precision is impossible
- **Biology**: Molecular detection has fundamental noise limits

### **A5: Biological Constraints (Metabolic Limits)**
```
ATP_consumption ≤ ATP_budget, [Tracer] ≤ [NOAEL]
```
- **Principle**: Living systems have finite metabolic capacity and toxicity limits
- **Implication**: Biological systems constrain allowable operations
- **Biology**: Must stay within cellular energy budgets and safety limits

   - Stays within cellular metabolic budgets

2. **Preserve Information** (A2) 
   - Neural activity information captured in calcium binding
   - Signal processing preserves information content

   - Diffusion speeds respect physical limits

4. **Accept Quantum Limits** (A4)

   - Shot noise from finite photon detection  
   - Quantum decoherence in biological environments

5. **Maintain Biocompatibility** (A5)
   - Toxicity below NOAEL limits (< 1 μM)
   - ATP depletion within physiological rates
   - No harmful neuroinflammation or cell death
